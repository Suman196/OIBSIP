Tribute to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015), better known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He played a pivotal role in India's nuclear weapons tests and made significant contributions to the development of India's space and missile programs.

Achievements

  • Played a key role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, the SLV-3.
  • Led the development of India's first indigenous guided missile program, resulting in the successful launch of the Prithvi missile.
  • Played a crucial role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests, which established India as a nuclear power.
  • Received several prestigious awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997.
  • Authored numerous books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," which continue to inspire millions.
